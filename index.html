CoRL 2026 half-day in-person workshop

Robotics World Models: Grounding, Evaluation, and Control

A working session on what robotics world models should represent, how they should be evaluated, and how they can be used safely inside robot learning and control loops.

Submission dates Contact organizers
Research Questions

Core challenges

Representation and grounding

Should models predict pixels, states, action-conditioned futures, latent dynamics, task progress, contact events, or a hybrid? How should actions, proprioception, force, language goals, and task context be grounded across embodiments?

Planning and control interfaces

How can world models support short-horizon correction and long-horizon task progress without unstable autoregressive error? When should a model signal completion, recovery, or uncertainty high enough to stop planning?

Evaluation beyond video quality

What demonstrates control utility rather than visual plausibility? Candidate criteria include task success under model-predictive control, 3D and physical consistency, contact prediction, model-policy feedback loops, and human judgments of task progress.

Community infrastructure

Which datasets, benchmarks, simulation rollouts, policy-in-the-loop protocols, and reporting standards will let the community compare robotics world models across manipulation, dexterous control, manufacturing, and other high-precision settings?

Submissions

Submission and reviewing process

We will invite full workshop papers of up to 8 pages and short submissions of 2-4 pages. Short submissions may focus on proof-of-concept results, benchmark proposals, system descriptions, demos, datasets, code, blog posts, negative results, or position papers.

Submissions will use the CoRL workshop format and will be managed through OpenReview with a double-blind review process.
